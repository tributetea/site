GREEN TEA

The best known types of tea are the three major varietal categories: green, oolong, and black. It is, in large part, the method used to process the leaves after harvesting which produces the dramatic differences we see in the finished products of the different tea varieties. The most noticeable distinction between teas is achieved through the process of oxidation which darkens the leaves and is responsible for the different appearances and flavor styles. Green tea, however, is unique in that it is not allowed to oxidize, and thus retains its bright green color and fresh, crisp taste. Instead, after plucking it is pan-fired, steamed or air dried to prevent oxidation and preserve its green properties.
