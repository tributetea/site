China's famous Yixing pottery (pronounced: "ee-shing") is produced in the town of the same name in the east coast province of Jiangsu. The clay produced there (known in Chinese as zisha - "purple sand") is left unglazed, allowing it to absord the color and flavor of the tea brewed in it. (For this reason, Yixing teapots are usually reserved for brewing only one kind of tea, i.e. black tea or oolong, and often for one specific tea, such as Yan Cha or Tieguanyin.)

At Tribute Tea, we do not purchase Yixingware in bulk; instead, we individually hand-select each piece, evaluating design, material, functionality, workmanship and overall value. There are many sources today for low-grade, novelty Yixing pots. Ours are selected by tea drinkers for tea drinkers - from the beginner to the serious collector.
