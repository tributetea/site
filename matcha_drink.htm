We are proud to present two special matchas grown outside the old imperial capital of Kyoto in Uji - long famous for its superior tea. Shading bushes for matcha production is believed to have begun here and Uji tea continues to be the most sought-after for connoisseurship and ceremonial use. After 3 weeks undercover the leaves are harvested, steamed and dried before being sorted and stone-ground.

The ultimate green tea... Special shade-grown tea plants are carefully cultivated and harvested and their leaves pulverized into a fine powder. Matcha, the tea of the Japanese "Tea Ceremony" is whisked with hot water and drunk as a frothy, deep green beverage. Because of its powdered form, matcha also has many culinary uses such as green tea ice cream, cheesecake, cookies, health drinks, tea lattes and frappuccinos.

THICK TEA - KOICHA

All matcha may be prepared as thin tea using 1-2 tsp. of tea per cup of water. The highest grades, however, may also be prepared as koicha ("thick tea") as shown above. Whisking 6-8 tsp. of matcha in a cup of water produces the ultimate bowl of green tea: the texture is thick and smooth, the color is intense and the flavor is truly unforgettable. Add a little ceremony to your next tea gathering. (For best results, sifting the tea in advance is recommended.)

To prepare Japanese Matcha, place approximately 1 teaspoon of powdered tea per cup (8 oz) and cover with hot (but less than boiling) water and whisk vigorously. To get a better mix, you can add just a bit of water to make a paste of the tea powder to start and then add the rest and stir well.
For culinary use, add to your favorite baking recipes for green tea color and flavor.
