Since the time of Japan's most famous tea master, Sen Rikyu (1522-1591), raku ware has been the favored teaware of wabi-cha - the rustic, Zen-influenced tea ceremony of Japan. Raku pottery is rapidly fired and then removed from the kiln while still glowing hot to cool. The result is the unique glossy black tea bowl that has been at the center of chanoyu for centuries.
